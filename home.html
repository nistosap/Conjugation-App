Verb types in the app

VAI: Animate Intransitive Verb: An animate subject doing an action. Example: I laughed.

VTA: Animate Transitive Verb: An animate subject doing an action on/with an animate object. Example: I am playing with my dog.
There are 4 types of VTA verbs.
VTA-2: verbs ending “aw”.
VTA-3: verbs ending in a consonant and the letter “w”.
VTA-4: verbs ending in the letter “t”.
VTA-1: all other verbs.

VTI: Inanimate Transitive Verb:An animate subject acting on/with an inanimate object. Example: The dog ate my book.
There are 3 types of VTI verbs.
VTI-1: verbs ending in the letter “a”.
VTI-2: verbs ending in the letter “â”.
VTI-3: all other verbs

Person

1: 1st person “I/Me”
2: 2nd person “You”
3: 3rd person “She/He or Her/Him” but more accurately it would be an animate object, animal, or person as there is no gender for conjugation
3'(P): 3rd person obviative - an animate object/animal/person in relation to a 3rd person - for example: my sister's friend(s), Bob's dog(s), your parent's pipe(s) - conjugation for both singular and plural forms are the same so I grouped them together.
1P: 1st person plural “We/Us” exclusive(exc) meaning the speaker and their party, but not the listener, are going to do an action
21: 2nd person inclusive “We/Us” inclusive(inc) meaning the speaker and their party, and the listener, are going to do an action together.
2P: 2nd person plural “You” or “you all”
3P: 3rd person plural “They/Them” or those animate objects, animals, or people.
s.o.: simply “someone” but more accurately it would be an animate object, animal, or person.

Grammar definitions

Imperative (Imp) mode is a command. When the speaker is telling someone to do something. Example: Go to sleep
verb pattern: preverb - verb + ending

Indicative (Ind) mode is a statement. When a speaker is making a statement. Example: I am eating.
verb pattern: person + tense - preverb - verb + ending

Conjunct (Con) clause is a clause that is not normally a full statement. Example: As I was cleaning, I saw something. “As I was cleaning” is the conjunct clause.
verb pattern: "ê-" + tense - preverb - verb + ending

Relative clause gives more information about the noun in the sentence. I use “who” in my English samples, but it can translated as “that/which/who/when/where” Examples: I like cats that purr. “That purr” is the relative clause. It was me who took the cookie. “who took” is the relative clause.
verb pattern: "kâ-" + tense - preverb - verb + ending

Infinitive clause is a verb that seems like the object of the sentence. Example: I like to work. “To work” is the infinitive clause.
verb pattern: "ta-" + tense - preverb - verb + ending

Inverse (Inv) mode is used when a speaker is describing a 3rd person (her/him/an animate thing) as the actor of the sentence, acting on/with another person/animate object. Example: She is dancing with me. She is dancing with you. She is dancing with them.
verb pattern: person + tense - preverb - verb + ending

You-Me (You) mode is used when a speaker is describing a 2nd person (you) as the actor of a sentence and the speaker is the object of the sentence. Example. You love me.
verb pattern: "ki(t)-" + tense - preverb - verb + ending

Verb tenses:

Present tense: Happening in the present moment.

Past tense: It happened before this present moment.

Future Indefinite (Fut Ind): It may happen. I am intending to do an action. I am going to do it.

Future Definite (Fut Def): It will happen. I will do it.

Future Conditional (Fut Con): If/when I eat, then I will be full. “If/when I eat” is the conditional verb.
